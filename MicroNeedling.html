Micro Needling

What is Micro Needling?

This technique involves the introduction of fine needles through the skin. The purpose is to create micro-channels in the skin allowing the infusion of active ingredients to penetrate deeply and effectively into the dermis, nourshing the skin and stimulating the regrowth of collagen.

Benefits

It reduces fine lines, wrinkles, enlarged pores and scarring.

Indications

Wrinkles, lax skin, stretch marks, scars.

Contra-indications

  • History of keloid or hypertrophic scars
  • Poor wound healing
  • Aids
  • Active cancer
  • Have open wounds, sores, cuts, abarasions, or irritated skin in the treatment area(s)
  • Have an allergy to stainless steel, or anesthetics
  • Have a hemorrhagic (bleeding) disorder or hemostatic (bleeding) dysfunction
  • Pregnant or nursing
  • Have any kind of current skin infection or condition, including but not limited to herpes, simplex
  • Currently on acne medications (that contains the ingredients isotretinoin)
  • Botox or fillers done up to 14 days prior to treatment
  • Allergy to any ingredients in the skincare brand used
  • Have had radiation treatment to the skin within the last year
  • Active breakouts or open lesions

Risks and side effects

Temporary brusing, skin discomfort during treatment, redness, swelling, lightening or darkening of the skin, itching, burning, skin infection. Side effects are most of the time temporary and typically resolve within 3 days. Total healing time depends on the depth of the treatment, skin type and skin condition, and some paitents may heal completely within 24 hours.

Frequency

A series of 4 to 6 treatments are recommended and the frequency will depend on the intensity and depth of the needle.

Steps

  1. A thorough skin consultation with a proposal treatment plan, including checking for contra-indications will be conducted.
  2. Your skin will be cleansed, degreased and appropriate serum will be applied.
  3. Needling will commence.
  4. Post treatment cream and SPF will be applied and aftercare will be discussed.

Pre Care

Avoid excessive sun exposure/burns/tanning beds prior to procedure and during treatment process. Discontinue use of topial retinoids 24 hours prior to procedure. Take anti-virals if you are prone to cold sores to avoid breakouts post treatment. Take Arnica 3 days prior to reduce swelling, bruising and pain. Use SPF daily. Avoid vitamin A, chemical peels, injectable fillers and botox 2 weeks prior to treatment. Wear comfortable clothing, your top should button or zip up rather than pull over the head.

Post Care

  • Cleanse with mild cleanser to remove serum and other debris such as dried up blood.
  • Do not use exfoliating products for 72 hours.
  • Cold compresses may be applied following treatment for comfort.
  • Apply aftercare product on treated areas to reduce itching or redness.
  • Continue taking Arnica Montana up to 7 days after each treatment to decrease brusing and inflammation.
  • Avoid saunas, steam rooms, hot baths or showers until redness is gone.
  • Continue to avoid sun exposure to treatment areas and apply SPF.
  • Apply SPF at least 30 minutes prior to sun exposure and repeat after every 2 hours of sun exposure.
  • After 2 to 3 days paitents can return to regular skin care products or as soon as it is comfortable to do so.
  • Mineral makeup maybe be applied to the following day.
  • Avoid strenuous excerise for 2 to 3 days after treatment.
  • Avoid waxing, facials, botox, injectable fillers or any other skin care treatment 2 weeks after treatment.
  • Drink water (for new cell regeneration).

Post treatment expectations

  • Day 1: Skin will be red and flushed after treatment, depending on the intensity of the treatment. Pinpoint bleeding may occur. Do not apply makeup for at least 12 hours.
  • Day 2: A red or pink hue persists like moderate sunburn. Swelling and slight brusing maybe be more noticeable on the 2nd day. Minor scratches may be visible. Apply aftercare products as needed.
  • Day 3: Skin can be pink or normal colour. Swelling subsides. The skin can feel dry or feel tight. A slight outbreak of acne or milla(tiny white bumps) is possible. Light peeling usually occurs in about 3 days and will be replaced with brand new skin.
  • Some paitents typically notice an immediate glow, but visible improvement will take about 2 to 4 weeks and can continue for up to 6 months.
