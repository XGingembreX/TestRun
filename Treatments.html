Laser Hair Removal

The laser emits a invisible highly concentrated, single wavelength beam of light. When the laser source is pulsed, the light energy is conducted as intense thermal heat. The pulse duration, spot size, and influence determine how much heat is absorbed by the mealanin in the hair folicle. The pulse of light is delievered just long enough to heat up the pigment, disabling the hair folicle to elimate or significantly impede the hair's growth. To impair the development of the stem cells within the bulge of the hair folicle & destruction of blood supply in the bulb is the key to permanent epilation. Darker hairs respond more effectively than lighter coloured hairs. Brown, dark drown and black are the best targets with the highest efficacy of clearance. Laser causes destruction of the hair folicle when the concentration of a pigment is higher in the hair folicle than the skin.

Dermaplaning

A form of mechanical exfoliation where a sterile blade is stroked gently along the skin to remove peach fuzz and "dead" skin cells revealing a smooth skin surface. Dermaplaning reduces the appearance of fine lines, assist in milla removal, reduce black and white heads, assist with underlying congestion, temporary removes peach fuzz, creates an immediate smooth texture and even skin tone, and removes "dead" skin/cells allowing for better product penetration.
