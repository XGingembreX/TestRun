Foot Peel

What is a Foot Peel?

Removal of dead skin cells and calluses from the feet, revealing softer and smoother feet.

Benefits

  • Exfoliate and soften skin
  • Reduce dryness
  • Improve skin texture
  • Dissolve hard, thickened skin associated with calluses
  • Nourishes and heals
  • Leaves skin soft, smooth and silky

Indications

Calloused feet. Dry, rough or cracked heels. General foot care.

Contra-indications

  • Open sores or wounds on your feet
  • Pregnancy, diabetes, eczema, psoriasis
  • Allergic to any of the ingredients on the label
  • Athlete's foot

Risks and side effects

Redness, swelling, itchiness, skin irritation, sensitivity, dry, flaky skin, worsening of any of the above symptoms in direct sunlight, allergic reaction/rash.

Frequency

Dependant on the individual and the severity of the calluses and dead skin on their feet.

Steps

  1. Feet will be sanitized (feet will be soaked and exfoliated and corn plane will be used).
  2. Peel will be applied and covered with plastic wrap and guaze for 10 minutes.
  3. Keratinised cells will be scraped off.
  4. Neutraliser shampoo will be applied and removed.
  5. Massage foot balm into feet.

Pre Care

Bring a pair of socks and closed shoes to avoid slipping. Feet must be clean, not sunburnt, infection-free and have no open wounds or sores.

Post Care

  • Gently wash and moisturise the treated area.
  • Avoid touching the treated area.
  • Avoid exfoliation.

Post treatment expectations

Softer, smoother, callous free feet.
