Dermaplaning

What is Dermaplaning?

A form of mechanical exfoliation where a sterile blade is stroked gently along the skin to remove peach fuzz and "dead" skin cells revealing a smooth skin surface. Dermaplaning reduces the appearance of fine lines, assist in milla removal, reduce black and white heads, assist with underlying congestion, temporary removes peach fuzz, creates an immediate smooth texture and even skin tone, and removes "dead" skin/cells allowing for better product penetration.

Benefits

  • Quick and relatively safe treatment.
  • Suitable for all skin types and Flitzpatrick photostypes.
  • No downtime.
  • Results are seen directly after treatment.
  • Safe whilst pregnant.
  • Will not induce thicker and darker hair growth.

Indications

Concerns of fine lines and wrinkles. Needing improvement of skin texture, smoothness and tone. Excessive facial hair. Enlarged and clogged pores.

Contra-indications

  • Active acne
  • Thick, dark hair
  • Chemotherapy
  • Skin cancer
  • Sunburn
  • Any active skin infection
  • Use of Roaccutan in the past year
  • Oral blood thinning medication
  • Scleroderma
  • Dermatitis

Risks & side effects

Redness, irritation, and dryness after treatment are possible. Blemishes and cold sores may appear post-treatment. There is a risk of scraping, nicking, or abrading the skin.

Steps

  1. A thorough skin consultation with a proposed treatment plan.
  2. Including checking for contra indications will be conducted.
  3. Your skin will be cleansed with a cleanser and an alcohol swab will be used to degrease your skin.
  4. A sterile blade will be stroked along your skin.
  5. Post treatment cream and SPF will be applied and aftercare will be discussed.

Pre Care

Avoid shaving and waxing for 7 days prior to treatment. Take an anti-viral 3 days prior to treatment if you are prone to cold sores.

Post Care

  • Cleanse your face with a mild cleanser and use a gentle moisturiser as needed.
  • Do not apply heavy makeup on the first day.
  • Avoid excessive sun exposure and make sure to use SPF.
  • Refrain from shaving and waxing for 7 days post-treatment.
  • Avoid vigorous activity until redness has subsided.
  • Do not use exfoliators for the first 3 days after treatment.
  • Do not pick, scratch, or rub the treated area.
  • Avoid excessive heat such as steam rooms or saunas.

Post treatment expectations

The skin may appear red and inflamed. Light scabs may form and remain for 24 to 48 hours. Slight peeling may occur. A slight windburn sensation or blotchiness is normal for the first few days.
