Laser Hair Removal

What is Laser Hair Removal?

The laser emits a invisible highly concentrated, single wavelength beam of light. When the laser source is pulsed, the light energy is conducted as intense thermal heat. The pulse duration, spot size, and influence determine how much heat is absorbed by the mealanin in the hair folicle. The pulse of light is delievered just long enough to heat up the pigment, disabling the hair folicle to elimate or significantly impede the hair's growth. To impair the development of the stem cells within the bulge of the hair folicle & destruction of blood supply in the bulb is the key to permanent epilation. Darker hairs respond more effectively than lighter coloured hairs. Brown, dark drown and black are the best targets with the highest efficacy of clearance. Laser causes destruction of the hair folicle when the concentration of a pigment is higher in the hair folicle than the skin.

How long does results last?

At any given time, a certain percentge of your hair will be in a "resting" phase. Hair removal is not effective on hair in a dormant phase. Due to variables of the numbers of hairs in the resting phase, certain parts of the body will respond more rapidly than others. Multiple treatments may be needed for desired amount of clearance. Due to hormonal or genetic influences some clients periodically need maintence treatments to maintain the improvement. With 4 to 6 laser treatments one can obtain a 50-60% hair reduction. 20-30% hair loss can be expected with each treatment. 90% is a long-term clearance rate. White, blonde, red and grey hair is difficult due to limited amounts of pigment. Coarser hair is easier to treat due to having a higher amount of pigment and a greater diameter shaft. All laser systems produce a temporary removal effect.

Contra-indications

  • History of skin cancer/suspicious lesion
  • Active infection
  • Diseases that may be stimulated by light e.g Lupus, Herpes Simplex
  • Photosensitizing medication e.g Isotretinoin, Tetracycline, Accutane
  • Immunosuppresive medication
  • Pregnancy and nursing
  • Active tanning
  • History of hormonal disorders e.g Uncontrolled Diabetes, PCOS
  • History of Keloid disorders
  • History of bleeding disorders
  • Use of anticoagulants

Risks and side effects

Possible side effects and potential complications include, but is not limited to:
  1. Pain, some discomfort can be felt during treatment. A warm sensation may last for an hour or two.
  2. Burn, there is a slight possibility of developing a crust, scab or blister. This is superficial and treated like a sunburn. The lesion could take 1 to 6 months to heal.
  3. Pigment changes, hypo- or hyperpigmentation is possible. These changes are temporary and should resolve within 3 to 6 months. Avoid sun exposure to not intensify the hyperpigmentation.
  4. Scarring, rare but compliance with reccomended aftercare guidelines is crucial for healing and the prevention of scarring and pigment disorders.
  5. Swelling, may occur around the hair follicles. This is temporary and not harmful.
  6. Fragile skin, skin should not be cared for gently for several days post treatment. Do not rub or pick at your skin.
  7. Bruising, rarely occur and will clear up within 4-10 days.
  8. Folliculitis, may occur after excessive sweating, swimming or exercise.
  9. Perifollicular oedema and Erythema are to be expected. This side effect indicates an appriopiate hair follicle response.
  10. Every client is an individual, we cannot guarantee that you will respond exactly the way other clients do. There will be variation in each response.

Discomfort

Some discomfort will be felt. Most clients tolerate the procedure quite easily. Pain Tolerance is subjective and is down to the individual. The sensation is like tweezing hair.

Steps

  1. Your therapist will explain the procedure to you and ask whether you have any concerns, questions, what types of medications you are on, and your medical history.
  2. Your goals will be confirmed. Photographs might be taken to document results.
  3. In the treatment room you will be wearing occlusive eyewear. if there are any visible hairs, it will be shaved. If you have tattoos, it will be covered.
  4. During treatment the continual assessment of your skin's reaction and tolerance will be conducted.
  5. Post treatment a sunburn sensation can be expected. SPF will be applied to relevant areas. After care will be explained.

Pre Care

Avoid sun exposure and use SPF 30 or higher. Do not pluck, wax, thread or epilate the hair at least 2 to 4 weeks prior to appointment. Shave treatment site 24 hours prior to treatment.

Post Care

  • Apply a wrapped ice pack or aloe gel to reduce discomfort or swelling as needed.
  • Cleanse skin gently.
  • Avoid scratching, picking, or rubbing the area.
  • Avoid tight clothing.
  • Moisturizing lotion can be used if the area becomes dry.
  • If crusting, scabbing or blister develop, apply a thin layer of Vaseline or antibiotic ointment to the site.
  • Re-apply as needed to prevent area from drying out. Please notifiy therapist if any blisters occur. Lesions may take 3 to 6 months to heal.
  • Use suncreen with SPF 30 or higher with UVA and UVB protection. Avoid prolonged sun exposure as it can cause hyperpigmentation.
  • For a more effective treatment result, only use an eletric razor. Do not pluck or wax the hairs in between appointments.
  • Make-up can be worn as soon as redness has diminished.

Post treatment expectations

Some redness may be seen. A sunburn feeling at the treatment site is normal. Swelling around the hair follicle is normal and can be relieved by applying an ice bad or aloe gel. Redness will disappear within the next few hours or the next day.

Ensure sucessful results

Attend sessions on time. Sessions should be attended at least every 4 to 6 weeks to achieve optimal results. Avoid shaving. Shaving can possibly aggravate hair growth. Rather use hair removal cream or a trimmer. Avoid hair growth medication. Hair growth medication can induce excessive hair growth in some clients. Speak to your therapist about alternatives. Attend maintence sessions when necessary. Follow prescribed aftercare advice.
